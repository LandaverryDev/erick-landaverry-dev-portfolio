## 2. About-Page Direction Draft

### Page Goal

The About page should explain how Erick works, what kinds of environments he has operated in, and why that matters to employers hiring for senior web developer, WordPress, or web leadership roles.

### Positioning Angle

Write the page as a practical professional summary, not a personal brand essay. The reader should understand that Erick can operate across the full website lifecycle: planning, build, optimization, integrations, governance, and ongoing support.

### Draft Direction

Erick is a senior web developer with deep WordPress experience, digital strategy awareness, and a strong full-site ownership mindset. He has worked in-house, with agencies, and in environments ranging from small business to enterprise. That range matters because it shows he can adapt to different teams, constraints, and delivery expectations without losing focus on quality.

He is strongest when the work needs more than a visual refresh. He brings technical judgment, frontend execution, and website operations thinking together so a site can support real business goals. That includes CRO, A/B testing with VWO, analytics and tag management, integrations, hosting, migrations, automations, and ongoing site governance.

He has also coordinated offshore and contractor developers on a project-by-project basis, with weekly standups, delivery planning, and integration oversight. That leadership experience should be described as project coordination and delivery ownership, not inflated people management.

The tone should stay direct, credible, and recruiter-friendly. It should make clear that Erick is comfortable being hands-on while still thinking about the website as a business tool.

### Suggested About-Page Structure

1. Short intro paragraph with the hybrid title and working philosophy.
2. Experience range paragraph covering in-house, agency, small business, and enterprise environments.
3. Leadership / ownership paragraph covering contractors, offshore contributors, and delivery coordination.
4. Website operations paragraph covering CRO, VWO, analytics, integrations, hosting, migrations, automations, ADA, and cookie compliance.
5. Closing paragraph that reaffirms full-site ownership and the kind of roles Erick is targeting.

### Draft Supporting Language

- `I build and maintain websites with a focus on performance, clarity, and business outcomes.`
- `My experience spans in-house teams, agencies, small business, and enterprise environments.`
- `I am comfortable working across design, development, analytics, integrations, and web operations.`
- `I have coordinated offshore and contractor developers on active projects with recurring standups and delivery planning.`
- `I care about websites that are fast, accessible, compliant, and easy to maintain.`

## 3. Resume-Page Narrative Direction

### Page Goal

The Resume page should feel like a polished, scannable extension of the portfolio. It should support recruiter review, reinforce seniority, and make Erick look organized, technical, and credible.

### Narrative Approach

The page should not read like a plain PDF replacement. It should turn the resume into a structured story about experience, leadership, technical breadth, and ownership.

### Recommended Story Arc

Start with a concise summary that reinforces Erick as a senior web developer with WordPress depth and digital strategy experience. Then move into the proof areas recruiters care about most:

- role progression
- scope of ownership
- technical platforms and integrations
- leadership of contractors or offshore contributors
- website operations and governance
- optimization, analytics, and conversion work

The resume page should make it easy to see that Erick has worked across different company sizes and operating environments and can contribute as both an individual contributor and a lead.

### What To Emphasize

- Full-site ownership
- WordPress depth
- frontend credibility
- digital strategy
- CRO and VWO testing
- GA4 and GTM integration
- hosting, deployments, and migrations
- ADA and cookie compliance ownership
- contractor and offshore coordination
- ability to work in-house, agency, small business, and enterprise contexts

### What To Avoid

- Overly long narrative blocks
- Buzzwords without proof
- Inflated manager language that is not accurate
- Generic resume filler
- PDF-only thinking

### Suggested Resume Page Sections

1. Hero summary with title and short positioning statement.
2. Quick proof strip with seniority themes.
3. Experience timeline with role, company, and scope.
4. Core strengths grouped by discipline.
5. Leadership and operations section.
6. Downloadable resume callout.
7. Contact CTA.

### Draft Resume Summary Language

`Senior web developer with deep WordPress experience, digital strategy depth, and a full-site ownership mindset. I have worked across in-house, agency, small business, and enterprise environments, with experience spanning frontend implementation, integrations, website governance, CRO, analytics, and delivery coordination.`

### Project Copy Rules

- Keep the summary short and specific.
- Show what Erick owned, not just what existed.
- Mention WordPress, frontend, integrations, or strategy where true.
- Use `Draft placeholder` labels anywhere facts are missing.
- Do not invent metrics, team size, or business outcomes.
- Let the strongest projects appear first on the site, but keep all 6 visible.

### Reusable Content Fields For Each Project

- title
- company or client
- role
- summary
- technologies
- ownership
- outcome
- notes

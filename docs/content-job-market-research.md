# Content Job Market Research

## Purpose

Ground the website and resume copy in current hiring signals for:

- Senior Frontend Developer
- WordPress Developer
- Web Development Manager
- Digital Strategy / Web Strategy roles

This research is used to shape positioning, not to invent experience. The site should reflect what companies are actually looking for, but every claim must still be true and supportable.

## High-Level Pattern

Current job listings consistently reward candidates who combine:

- hands-on frontend execution
- performance and accessibility awareness
- ownership of web experiences end to end
- collaboration across design, product, marketing, and engineering
- analytics and optimization fluency
- ability to connect strategy with implementation

For Erick, the strongest message is not just "frontend developer." The stronger marketable angle is:

- web leader with strong frontend and WordPress depth
- capable of owning a website end to end
- comfortable with integrations, analytics, SEO, and performance
- able to manage contractors or offshore contributors when needed
- credible in both execution and strategy conversations

## What Frontend Listings Emphasize

Across current senior frontend postings, common requirements include:

- React and modern JavaScript / TypeScript
- performance optimization
- responsive design
- accessibility
- reusable component systems
- testing and clean maintainable code
- working closely with design and product
- shipping polished production interfaces

Implication for copy:

- Erick should be presented as someone who builds high-quality frontend experiences, not just someone who "edits websites."
- The copy should reference performance, implementation quality, responsiveness, and production ownership where true.
- The portfolio should show that the work is both visual and technical.

## What WordPress / Website Ownership Roles Emphasize

Current web strategy and WordPress-adjacent roles emphasize:

- website ownership across multiple priorities
- CMS and content management discipline
- SEO and technical SEO awareness
- analytics and tracking
- conversion improvement
- integrations and operations
- cross-functional coordination
- execution plus roadmap thinking

Implication for copy:

- Erick should be framed as more than a theme implementer.
- The site should highlight full-site ownership, integrations, analytics awareness, and digital strategy contributions.
- WordPress depth should be treated as a core strength, not a side note.

## What Manager / Strategy Listings Emphasize

Manager and web strategy roles frequently ask for:

- roadmap ownership
- stakeholder communication
- leading redesigns and launches
- analytics and KPI fluency
- SEO / AEO / discoverability thinking
- CRO or optimization mindset
- managing developers, vendors, or agencies
- balancing strategy with hands-on execution

Implication for copy:

- Erick should be positioned as someone who can own the website as a business asset, not only as a developer.
- Leadership should be described through scope and decision-making, not through inflated titles.
- Offshore and contractor management should be included where relevant and factual.

## Recruiter-Friendly Themes To Emphasize

The site copy should repeatedly reinforce these themes where accurate:

- full-site ownership
- frontend and WordPress depth
- technical implementation plus strategy
- integrations and systems thinking
- performance and user experience
- experience across agency, in-house, small business, and enterprise contexts
- ability to manage distributed contributors

## Themes To Avoid

Avoid copy that sounds generic, inflated, or AI-generated:

- vague claims about passion or innovation
- filler like "results-driven" without proof
- overly polished brand language without specifics
- fake metrics or unsupported leadership claims
- making Erick sound purely managerial if the strength is hybrid ownership

## Recommended Positioning Direction

Best-fit positioning for the site:

- primary: web leadership grounded in web development
- supporting: senior frontend and WordPress depth
- supporting: digital strategy, analytics, and ownership mindset

This means the homepage should not read like:

- only a frontend engineer portfolio
- only a WordPress freelancer portfolio
- only a management resume

It should read like a hybrid operator who can lead, build, and improve a website across technical and business needs.

## Current Owner Inputs To Reflect

- Preferred title direction: hybrid, not narrow
- One-line summary direction: full-stack developer working on frontend and backend across enterprise products and tech stacks
- Strongest themes: WordPress depth, digital strategy, full-site ownership
- Leadership scope to mention: managed offshore developers and contractors on a project-by-project basis with recurring standups / scrum calls and integration work under legal or compliance constraints
- Contact methods: email, LinkedIn, and form
- Testimonials: placeholders for now
- Resume emphasis: a mix of leadership, technical depth, ownership, and strategy

## Constraint

The content writer can research hiring language and common expectations, but must not translate that into invented claims. If a skill or scope area is not real, it should not appear as if it is.

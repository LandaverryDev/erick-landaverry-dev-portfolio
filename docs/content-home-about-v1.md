## 2. About Page Draft

### About Page Goal

The About page should explain how I work, what I own, and why that matters to employers hiring for senior web, WordPress, lead, or digital strategy roles.

The tone should be practical and recruiter-friendly. It should show technical judgment, ownership, and range without sounding inflated.

### Section 1: Intro

I am a senior web developer with deep WordPress experience, a strong front-end foundation, and a full-site ownership mindset. I work best on websites that need more than visuals. I bring together development, strategy, operations, and optimization so a site can support business goals, not just look polished.

### Section 2: Experience Range

My background spans in-house teams, agencies, small business environments, and enterprise-level web work. That range matters because it means I can adapt to different processes, constraints, and expectations while still keeping delivery moving.

I have worked across frontend and backend priorities, website operations, and cross-functional coordination. I am comfortable contributing as an individual developer while also helping guide the bigger picture when a site needs leadership and structure.

### Section 3: Ownership And Delivery

I like owning the full life cycle of a website: planning, implementation, optimization, and ongoing support. That includes CRO, A/B testing with VWO, GA4 and GTM integrations, hosting, migrations, automations, and the kind of site governance that keeps projects stable after launch.

I also care about the operational details that keep a site usable and compliant. That includes ADA accessibility responsibility, cookie consent upkeep, and making sure user opt-in and opt-out preferences are handled correctly as requirements change.

### Section 4: Team Coordination

I have coordinated offshore and contractor developers on a project-by-project basis. That work included weekly standups or scrum calls, delivery planning, and integration oversight so contributors stayed aligned with the project and with the systems the site needed to connect to.

This is not about overstating formal people management. It is about real delivery ownership, communication, and keeping work moving in a structured way.

### Section 5: Practical AI Experience

I also have practical experience with AI in a business setting. My current company encouraged the use of ChatGPT Enterprise, and I was assigned as the AI champion for the marketing team. That work was focused on practical adoption, workflow support, and helping the team use the tool responsibly and effectively.

### Section 6: What I Focus On

The work I care about most is the kind that combines technical depth with business value:

- WordPress depth
- full-site ownership
- digital strategy
- frontend craft
- CRO and testing
- integrations and analytics
- migrations and hosting
- automations
- compliance and accessibility

### Section 7: Closing Paragraph

I am looking for roles where I can contribute as a senior web developer, WordPress specialist, web development lead, or digital strategy partner. The strongest fit is a team that needs someone who can own the website, communicate clearly, and keep the work grounded in performance, usability, and measurable outcomes.

# Homepage Wireframe Spec

## Purpose

Define the structure and intent of the homepage before high-fidelity design or frontend implementation begins.

This homepage should work as the main hiring surface for the site. It must quickly establish Erick’s positioning, credibility, range, and next-step actions.

## Page Goal

The homepage should answer these questions within the first scroll:

- Who is Erick?
- What kind of work does he lead and ship?
- Why is he credible?
- What work should I look at next?
- How do I contact him?

## Layout Order

1. Header / navigation
2. Hero
3. Capability / trust strip
4. Featured projects
5. Experience preview
6. Skills preview
7. Testimonials
8. Resume preview
9. Contact CTA / minimal form
10. Footer

## 1. Header / Navigation

### Purpose

Make key destinations obvious without crowding the top of the page.

### Elements

- Logo or name mark
- Projects
- Experience
- Resume
- Contact
- Mobile menu trigger

### Notes

- Keep the nav lean.
- Resume should be visible in nav.
- Contact should be visible in nav.
- Navigation should feel premium and light, not heavy or corporate.

## 2. Hero

### Purpose

Establish Erick’s positioning immediately.

### Content Direction

- A role statement that leans toward web leadership and digital strategy while staying grounded in web development.
- A short supporting paragraph that mentions:
  - frontend depth
  - WordPress experience
  - full-site ownership
  - work across in-house, agency, small business, and enterprise environments
- Two actions:
  - primary: view projects
  - secondary: contact

### Visual Direction

- Strong mixed-surface treatment
- Slightly more atmospheric than the rest of the page
- Subtle motion or layered visual detail
- Room for dummy portrait or supporting visual asset

### Layout Notes

- Keep the content tight and readable.
- Do not overload the hero with too many badges or statistics.
- The hero should feel confident and technical.

## 3. Capability / Trust Strip

### Purpose

Give immediate proof after the hero without forcing the user to read a long paragraph.

### Content Types

- Frontend
- WordPress
- Leadership
- Integrations
- Ownership

### Format Options

- short labeled highlights
- compact stat or proof blocks
- capability tags with short descriptions

### Notes

- This section should be fast to scan.
- Keep it structured and credible.

## 4. Featured Projects

### Purpose

Move the user into proof quickly.

### Requirements

- Show strongest projects first
- Support all 6 launch projects in the overall system
- Emphasize the most important projects visually without hiding the others

### Card Content

- Title
- Short summary
- Role
- Core stack
- Outcome or impact

### Layout Notes

- Cards should feel like evidence, not promo tiles.
- The section should be strong enough to hold recruiter attention.
- Mobile layout must remain easy to scan.

## 5. Experience Preview

### Purpose

Show work history and breadth without sending users straight to a full resume wall.

### Structure

- short intro
- timeline preview or structured role blocks
- link to full experience or resume page if needed

### Notes

- Keep this section compact.
- Highlight scope, leadership, integrations, and technical range.

## 6. Skills Preview

### Purpose

Support technical credibility through structure, not through a giant list.

### Grouping

- Frontend
- WordPress
- Integrations
- Tooling
- Leadership / strategy

### Notes

- The goal is to show breadth and relevance.
- Avoid visual clutter.

## 7. Testimonials

### Purpose

Add supporting proof from others.

### Structure

- 3 testimonial cards
- short quote
- name / role / company when available

### Notes

- Use placeholders until real testimonials are added.
- The section should feel restrained, not sales-heavy.

## 8. Resume Preview

### Purpose

Support resume-minded reviewers without making the homepage feel like a document.

### Structure

- short summary of resume value
- small preview of structured sections
- clear download CTA
- path to resume page

### Notes

- Do not embed the PDF here.
- Treat this as a preview, not a replacement for the resume page.

## 9. Contact CTA / Minimal Form

### Purpose

Make it easy to take action without requiring a separate search for contact details.

### Required Fields

- Name
- Email
- Message

### Supporting Elements

- Direct email
- LinkedIn

### Notes

- The section should feel simple and frictionless.
- It should integrate cleanly with a WordPress plugin such as WPForms.

## 10. Footer

### Purpose

Close the page cleanly and reinforce important paths.

### Elements

- Name or mark
- Secondary nav links
- Contact methods

## Motion Guidance

Apply motion sparingly and intentionally:

- hero entrance
- staggered section reveals
- project hover states
- CTA interaction feedback

Motion must:

- feel polished
- stay fast
- respect reduced-motion
- remain lighter on mobile

## Mobile Behavior

The homepage must feel fully designed on mobile.

### Mobile Priorities

- stacked hero with strong spacing
- readable capability strip
- project cards that remain clear at small sizes
- easy form interaction
- simplified but intentional section transitions

### Avoid

- cramped cards
- over-animated mobile sections
- giant paragraphs
- tiny tap targets

## Design Quality Bar

The homepage succeeds if it feels:

- lean
- modern
- technical
- credible
- easy to scan
- strong enough to win a second click
